# Curio — Competitive Landscape & Edge Strategy
*Researched and fact-checked 2026-07-12. Every load-bearing claim below was
independently verified against primary sources (verdicts in Appendix A).*

## The headline finding

**Almost every incumbent visibly retreated from "free" in 2022–2026:**

- **Quizlet** paywalled its previously-free Learn mode & practice tests
  (Aug 2022, $35.99/yr), restricted flashcard export → student boycotts. ✔ verified
- **GeoGuessr** ended free play (Feb 2024; unlimited free actually died ~2019),
  citing Google Street View licensing costs; its 2025 Steam launch was review-bombed
  over "predatory monetization." ✔ verified
- **Kahoot** cut its free plan to **10 players/game** and paywalled reports;
  paid tiers $3–19/mo. ✔ verified
- **Duolingo** replaced Hearts with an "Energy" meter (July 2025) that limits
  free users to ~2–4 lessons/day; Super ≈ $84–96/yr, Max ≈ $168/yr. ✔ verified

**Conclusion: "Free forever, no ads, no paywalled knowledge" is not a soft
brand value — it is a defensible market wedge, because the entire market has
abandoned that ground.**

## The cautionary tale: QuizUp ✔ verified

Peaked at ~80–100M registered users, NBC show cancelled 2016, sold to Glu for
just $7.5M, shut down early 2021. Founder's own words: *"We never managed to
monetise properly."*
**Lesson: engagement is not a business model. Stand up ethical revenue
(patronage + cosmetics + B2B) BEFORE chasing scale, and keep serving costs
near zero** (text questions, static hosting, no per-use licensing — Curio
already satisfies this; GeoGuessr's fatal flaw was per-use Street View costs).

### The two "closest threats" are tiny (verified — good news)
- **SAPIRO** (iOS/Android, Feb 2026): explanation-per-answer positioning
  overlaps Curio's depth facts — but ~7 ratings, ~100+ downloads, no
  independent coverage. Real but marketing-inflated. ✔ verified
- **LearnClash** (iOS/Android, Feb–Mar 2026): 1v1 duels + 3-stage spaced
  repetition + ELO, no ads — solo developer, ~370 lifetime downloads. ✔ verified

**Read on timing:** two indie devs just launched into this exact niche in
early 2026 — the gap is being noticed *now*. The white space
(free + broad general knowledge + real retention + polish + ethical model)
is real and essentially unoccupied at scale. Move fast.

## Edge features, ranked (what gives Curio its hedge)

1. **Memory Vault** — spaced repetition on facts you missed, woven INTO the
   daily challenge (no separate flashcard chore). *Anki's science without
   Anki's ugliness; Quizlet paywalled exactly this.*
2. **Depth-fact Rabbit Holes** — every answer's fact links to 2–3 connected
   facts; you build a browsable web of what you've learned. *Nobody does the
   connected-trail.*
3. **Fact vs. Fake mode** — a game mode training source-literacy; every Curio
   fact carries a citation. *Mission-critical (the "stupidity epidemic"),
   unowned by any competitor, and grant-fundable: 68% of people say they want
   help distinguishing true from false online (BU/Media literacy survey 2024).*
4. **Curio Nights (pub-quiz mode)** — host a live group quiz free, no player
   cap. *Kahoot just cut free to 10 players; the dinner-party/pub niche is open.*
5. **Spoiler-free shareable daily result** — already built (emoji grid). Keep
   polishing; it's the zero-cost growth engine (Wordle's proven loop).
6. **Community question studio with credibility scoring** — user-submitted
   questions + sources, reputation-gated review. *Free content scale WITH a
   quality filter; Sporcle/Quizlet UGC is unvetted.*
7. **Knowledge-graph profile** — "your brain, visualized": a living map of
   domains mastered vs. gaps. *Makes being a nerd tangible, shareable, sexy.*
8. **Learning-tuned Elo** — per-domain difficulty rating that serves questions
   at the edge of your ability (desirable difficulty), not just fair matches.
9. **Retention streaks** — streaks that reward *still knowing* things
   (surprise re-tests), not just opening the app. *Anti-Duolingo-streak-decay.*
10. **Deep Dives** — multi-day themed journeys ("The Silk Road") blending
    quiz + narrative. *Brilliant's depth, humanities breadth, free.*
11. **Co-op boss quizzes** — friends pool knowledge across domains. *All
    competitors are zero-sum; co-op knowledge play is unexplored.*
12. **"Explain it back"** — occasional free-recall (type the answer) before
    showing options. *The testing effect; nobody in the category does it.*
13. **B2B pack studio** — teachers/quiz hosts/companies build custom Curio
    packs (paid). *The revenue engine; never touches consumer free tier.*
14. **Offline-first everywhere** — already built (PWA). Low-bandwidth is a
    moat, not a cost, because our content is text.
15. **Nerd credentials** — cosmetic titles/badges/themes ("Cartographer",
    "Polymath"). *Monetizable without gating a single fact.*

**Recommended build order for v1.1–v2:** #1 Memory Vault → #2 Rabbit Holes →
#4 Curio Nights → #3 Fact vs. Fake. (#5 and #14 already shipped in MVP/PWA.)

## Ethical monetization playbook (verified models)

| Model | Proof | What Curio copies |
|---|---|---|
| **Wikipedia** | FY2023-24: $185.4M revenue, ~$174.7M donations, avg gift ~$10, zero ads ✔ verified | Small-donor patronage at scale + a B2B arm (Wikimedia Enterprise) that never touches the free product |
| **Signal** | Nonprofit, donations + grants, no ads/tracking | The trust story: "sustained by the people who value it" — a dignified in-app Support flow |
| **Khan Academy** | <1% of users donate; grants carry the rest | You don't need many payers; diversify with institutional money |
| **Anki** | Free everywhere; one-time $24.99 iOS app funds the whole ecosystem | One-time purchases (cosmetics/supporter) funding free-for-all |
| **Kahoot (ethical half)** | Schools/companies pay for hosting tools | B2B pack studio & quiz-night tools — institutions pay, individuals never |
| Duolingo (anti-model) | Ads + throttled free tier | Copy the gamification craft; reject the wall. Pitch: "Duolingo's fun, without the wall." |

**Curio revenue stack, in rollout order:**
1. Supporter/patronage tier (Signal/Wikipedia style) — v2
2. Cosmetic "nerd credentials" — v2
3. B2B: Curio for Schools / Quiz Hosts / Companies — v3
4. Institutional grants — the Fact-vs-Fake media-literacy angle is directly
   fundable by education/democracy foundations — apply once #3 feature exists
